Korean Thanksgiving

One of the most important Korean holidays, celebrating the autumn harvest and honoring ancestors.

Traditions:

  • Family reunions and ancestral rituals
  • Traditional foods like songpyeon (rice cakes)
  • Visiting family graves
  • Three-day holiday period

Korean New Year celebration based on the lunar calendar, marking new beginnings and family unity.

Customs:

  • Sebae (formal bows to elders)
  • Traditional hanbok clothing
  • Tteokguk (rice cake soup)
  • Ancestral memorial services

  • Vibrant colors with symbolic meanings
  • Modern adaptations for daily wear

Modern Korean Culture

Contemporary Korea blends traditional values with modern innovation, creating a unique cultural landscape that has captivated the world.

üéµ

Hallyu (Korean Wave)

The global phenomenon of Korean popular culture, including K-pop, K-dramas, and Korean cinema.

Key Elements:

  • K-pop groups and solo artists
  • Korean dramas (K-dramas)
  • Korean movies and webtoons
  • Korean beauty and fashion

Technology & Innovation

Korea is a global leader in technology, with high smartphone adoption and digital connectivity.

Cultural Impact:

  • Delivery culture and convenience
  • Digital payment systems
  • Online gaming and esports
  • Social media and streaming

Education Culture

Education is highly valued, with intense focus on academic achievement and continuous learning.

Characteristics:

  • Respect for teachers and learning
  • Competitive academic environment
  • Private tutoring culture (hagwon)
  • Emphasis on hard work and dedication
üíº

Work Culture

Korean work culture emphasizes dedication, teamwork, and building relationships with colleagues.

Features:

  • Company loyalty and team spirit
  • After-work socializing (hoesik)
  • Hierarchical workplace structure
  • Growing work-life balance awareness
